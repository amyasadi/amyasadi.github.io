Converging Service Design with Ecosystem Thinking

Redesigning SEPTA Regional Rail App for multiple red-routes

Introduction

This is the design project I chose to take on for my Design for Mobile Spaces course at Moore College of Art & Design.

Discovery

Unveiling the Commuter Ecosystem through Ethnography

For this project I chose to do hands-on field research in the form of ethnographic interviews. This enabled me to objectively study the ‘messy reality” (what people actually do versus what they say they do) within the Septa user natural ecosystem.

After a series of interviews, I was able to design an ecosystem map based on the habitat of several Regional Rail users.

Interaction designer, Sofia Hussain, wrote a great article on ecosystem thinking. Essentially ecosystem maps help us answer the 5 W + H questions: who, what, where, when, why and how.

Red-Route Mapping (Feature Prioritization)

Red-Route Mapping is a great technique for assisting with issues like product backlogs. They can also help us focus on users' top tasks. I found that Regional Rail riders have two main top-tasks: viewing a schedule and finding the next train. Below is also a red-route map of prioritized sub-tasks I considered when designing for this ecosystem.

Ideation

Navigating Through Fight or Flight

Since ecosystem thinking is holistic in nature, I paired storyboarding with IA patterns to better predict how the user will use the application in their ecosystem.

Because the user is often in motion, it was important for me to layout information that is fast and efficient to get to. I chose a hub and spoke model because I found it to be both ergonomic and predictable, as this model is often used for mobile devices because it takes up little space. The menu can also fall nicely in the natural flow of a user’s thumb and touch points. I found this layout to be accessible for both right and left hand users.
